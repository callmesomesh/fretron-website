What Is A Yard Management System?

Yard management systems (YMS) are tools developed to assist organizations in overseeing their yard trucks, trailers, containers, and additional assets across their yards and exterior storage spaces. These systems enhance the transparency of yard management activities and enable companies to refine their yard operations, cut expenses, and boost productivity.

How Is A Yard Management System (YMS) Different From A Warehouse Management System (WMS)?

Although both systems may appear to be similar at first glance, there are several distinct differences between them. Let's explore these differences:

  1. Operational Scope

A significant distinction between YMS and WMS lies in the breadth of operations they oversee. WMS focuses on the inner workings of a warehouse, managing tasks such as inventory management, and the processes of picking, packing, and dispatching goods. It is designed to streamline the movement of goods within the warehouse, from the point of receipt to the point of shipment.

Conversely, YMS is concerned with external activities outside the warehouse. It is tailored for use in locations like distribution centers, ports, and manufacturing facilities, where it manages the transportation of goods in trailers and enhances the efficiency of moving goods into and out of such areas.

  1. Data Gathering

There's also a difference in the type of data that YMS and WMS gather and analyze. YMS focuses on tracking the movement of trailers within the yard, capturing information on their location, status, and how long they've been parked. It can also monitor the usage of equipment and assets like forklifts and yard trucks, using this information to refine yard operations, diminish waiting times, and boost overall productivity.

In contrast, WMS collects data concerning inventory, such as the amount of stock, the locations of items, and how orders are filled. This information is crucial for enhancing the accuracy of inventory records, minimizing out-of-stock scenarios, and improving the rate at which orders are completed.

  1. Cost of Implementation

The cost of implementing a YMS tends to be lower than that of setting up a WMS. The simpler nature of YMS, with its focused aim on regulating the movement of goods in and out of the yard, results in fewer requirements and, consequently, lower costs. WMS, with its extensive range of functions including the management of goods' movement within a warehouse or distribution center, is more intricate and costlier to implement. This complexity often necessitates integration with additional systems such as Enterprise Resource Planning and Customer Relationship Management systems to ensure the warehouse operations are in harmony with other business segments.

How Is A Yard Management System Different From A Transport Management System?

Both these systems are pivotal in the orchestration of logistics operations, yet they diverge significantly in their methodologies and functionalities. Here's a closer examination of these distinctions:

  1. Operational Scope

The Yard Management System (YMS) is specialized for overseeing the yard area of a warehouse, including the tracking of trailer and container movements, yard space management, and facilitating coordination among drivers, dispatchers, and warehouse staff.

Conversely, the Transportation Management System (TMS) extends its reach to encompass transportation activities across various locations. It oversees the complete supply chain journey, from the dispatch of goods from the warehouse to their final delivery to the customer. Its responsibilities encompass route planning, selecting carriers, scheduling shipments, and optimizing loads.

  1. Interactions with Customers

The TMS encompasses features that enable customers to engage with their transportation service or carrier and monitor their shipment's progress. These capabilities often involve online booking, real-time shipment tracking, and receiving delivery updates, thus offering enhanced visibility into the transportation sequence.

Conversely, the YMS is more internally focused, primarily serving yard staff like yard managers and jockeys, without incorporating customer engagement features. Given its concentration on yard operations, the aspect of customer interaction is less pertinent in YMS compared to TMS.

  1. Cost of Implementation

Implementing a TMS tends to be costlier than YMS due to its broader and more intricate management of transportation networks. The complexity of TMS necessitates additional software functionalities and capabilities, which are superfluous for YMS. Furthermore, the integration, support, and ongoing maintenance required for TMS contribute to its higher implementation cost.

How Does a YMS Enhance the Functionality of WMS and TMS?

Let's delve into how a Yard Management System (YMS) enhances both a Warehouse Management System (WMS) and a Transportation Management System (TMS) for a more efficient logistics operation.

  1. Advantages of Merging YMS with WMS

While a WMS excels in overseeing the internal operations of a warehouse, it lacks visibility over the activities occurring in the yard. This gap is filled by a YMS, which extends the visibility of a WMS to include the status and whereabouts of the inventory situated in the yard. This insight is crucial because inventory located in the yard is not available for order fulfillment until it has been moved into the warehouse.

By implementing a YMS, warehouse managers gain the ability to monitor the status of trailers and containers in the yard, organize their movements, and ensure timely transportation into the warehouse.

  1. Advantages of Merging YMS with TMS

The integration of a YMS with a TMS enhances the management of trailer and container movements from the yard to the carrier, ensuring that they are loaded onto the appropriate carrier at the correct time, thereby optimizing transportation operations.

Furthermore, this integration grants transportation managers improved visibility and instant access to information regarding the position and condition of trailers and containers in the yard, aiding in the prevention of mistakes and delays in the transportation process.

Having introduced the basic concepts of YMS and its integration benefits with TMS and WMS, let's not conclude here. To fully understand the subject, it's crucial to familiarize oneself with the features of YMS and how they contribute to streamlining operations. Let's explore further.

Advantages Of Yard Management System In 5 Key Operational Areas

The emergence of eCommerce and growing customer expectations for rapid delivery have intensified the pressure on firms to enhance their yard management operations. This overview highlights the advantages of implementing a yard management system across five critical areas of yard operations.

1. Truck and Trailer Management Enhancement through Yard Management Systems

Efficiently handling trucks and trailers is crucial in the supply chain, with complexities that can lead to delays affecting the entire network. Yard management technologies streamline these processes in several ways:

  • Enhanced Operational Efficiency: Automating yard management tasks reduces the need for manual labor and boosts efficiency. Technologies like real-time location tracking simplify parking and speed up yard entry and exit, minimizing delays.
  • Shorter Waiting Periods: Yard management systems alleviate long wait times by optimizing vehicle flow, thanks to automated gate procedures and quick identification of congestion points.
  • Improved Relations with Carriers: Enhanced efficiency and reduced waiting times bolster relationships with carriers, making your operation more appealing and potentially increasing business.
  • Increased Safety and Security: Implementing yard management technologies ensures better control over site access and minimizes human error, enhancing overall safety and security.
  • Direct Communication with Carriers: Providing carriers with up-to-date information on truck and trailer status improves clarity and strengthens partnerships.

2. Dock Management Benefits from Yard Management Systems

Incorporating dock management into yard management software offers significant advantages, such as:

  • Minimized Dwell Times: Scheduling docks through the system decreases loading and unloading wait times, improving productivity.
  • Enhanced Visibility: Real-time insights into dock and yard space usage facilitate more informed decisions, optimizing resource allocation.
  • Efficient Resource Allocation: Dock management software aids in the effective distribution of dock and yard resources, enhancing utilization and productivity.
  • Quicker Turnaround Times: Streamlining dock operations shortens dwell times, enabling faster goods movement and better customer satisfaction.

3. Inventory Management Improvements with Yard Management Systems

Yard management solutions significantly enhance inventory oversight and efficiency through:

  • Accurate Inventory Tracking: Real-time tracking with RFID technology ensures precise inventory location and movement records.
  • Optimized Space Usage: The system allows for better yard space utilization, reducing the risks associated with overcrowding or underuse.
  • Lower Inventory Shrinkage: Integrating the yard with warehouse management systems decreases the chances of inventory loss, mitigating theft risks.
  • Balanced Inventory Levels: Monitoring inventory and demand helps avoid stockouts and excesses, maintaining customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

4. Advantages for Yard Staff Management through Yard Management Systems

The yard staff management module offers benefits such as:

  • Streamlined Operations: Automating workflows reduces manual tasks, allowing staff to concentrate on more critical duties.
  • Lower Manual Labor Needs: Automation of routine tasks decreases labor costs and reallocates staff to other areas.
  • Enhanced Communication and Coordination: Real-time operation updates improve staff collaboration, reducing errors and boosting productivity.
  • Reduced Overtime: Efficiency gains from streamlined operations lessen the necessity for overtime, saving costs and enhancing staff satisfaction.

5. Yard Equipment Management Optimization with Yard Management Systems

Effective equipment management contributes to more efficient yard operations in several ways:

  • Better Equipment Use: Monitoring and planning equipment usage minimizes idle time and maximizes operational productivity.
  • Less Equipment Downtime: Maintaining equipment according to a set schedule reduces breakdowns, keeping operations smooth.
  • Lower Equipment Expenses: Insight into equipment utilization helps make informed decisions on equipment needs, avoiding unnecessary purchases.
