What is Digital Indent Allocation in Logistics and Supply Chain Management?

Indent allocation in logistics and supply chain management refers to coordinating vehicle allocation for shipments. Essentially, it's when the logistics team makes a request, or 'indent,' to a contracted transporter for vehicle placement. This is done following existing freight contracts.

This system exists to guarantee the timely placement of vehicles for every shipment. The aim is to prevent any delays in the supply chain that could occur due to vehicle unavailability or indent cancellations.

Why is Digital Indent Allocation Gaining Popularity?

Indent allocation plays a crucial role in logistics, driven by numerous business objectives:

Ensuring timely vehicle availability

Businesses need a system to predict when a vehicle will be required and ensure it's available at the right time. The ability to coordinate this effectively can lead to significant improvements in efficiency and cost-effectiveness.

Vehicle assignments based on real-time demand

Sometimes, the need for a vehicle might arise suddenly or unexpectedly. A system can respond to real-time changes and quickly assign vehicles in these instances.

Instantaneous collaboration and updates

Logistics involves coordinating many different elements. A digital indent allocation system allows for real-time updates and collaboration between all parts of the supply chain, enabling more effective decision-making and quicker responses to any changes.

With these demands, it becomes clear why a system that can simplify and streamline the indent allocation process is necessary. This is where digital indent allocation comes into the picture.

How does Digital Indent Allocation work in Logistics?

The Indent Allocation System follows a structured approach to effectively manage vehicle placement for shipments. Let's break down how it typically works:

Indent Creation:

When a shipment is ready, the logistics team creates an indent (a formal request) for a vehicle from a contracted transporter. This indent includes necessary details such as the type of vehicle required, the pickup and drop-off locations, and the scheduled time for vehicle placement.

Transporter Confirmation:

Upon receiving the indent, the transporter checks the availability of vehicles that match the request's specifications. Once a suitable vehicle is identified, the transporter confirms the indent and schedules the vehicle for pickup.

Vehicle Placement:

The transporter ensures that the confirmed vehicle arrives at the pickup location on the scheduled date and time. The shipment is then loaded onto the vehicle for delivery.

Real-time Updates and Tracking:

Throughout this process, updates are sent to the logistics team. This includes the status of the indent request, confirmation from the transporter, and the current location of the vehicle.

Indent Closure:

Once the shipment is successfully delivered at the drop-off location, the indent is closed. The transporter submits a proof of delivery, which is verified by the logistics team. After verification, the indent is officially closed in the system.
